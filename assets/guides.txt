### **Symptom 1: Unit is Listing / Swimming Upside Down**

**Diagnosis:** Cryo-Bladder Dysfunction.
The gas bladder often fails to re-pressurize immediately after waking from cryo-stasis, causing the whale to float belly-up. This is common and usually resolves in 2 hours.

**Corrective Actions:**

1. **Do Not Poke:** Physical correction stresses the animal.
2. **Increase Flow:** Turn your tank's wave-maker to "Storm Mode" (High Turbulence). The chaos encourages the unit to engage its core stabilizers.
3. **Salinity Adjustment:** If the issue persists > 4 hours, your water density may be too low. Increase salinity to **1.026 SG**.

---

### **Symptom 2: "Glass Surfing" (Pacing the Tank Walls)**

**Diagnosis:** Boundary Mapping Stress.
The unit is confused by the transparency of the glass and is looking for the rest of its pod.

**Corrective Actions:**

1. **Block Line of Sight:** Tape paper to the outside of the tank for 24 hours to create a visual barrier.
2. **Run Diagnostic Script:**
Check if your tank's ultrasonic barrier is active. If you have the `orca-cli` installed, run this bash command to verify the sonic containment field is on:
```bash
# Check status of the glass-repellent emitters
./orca-cli sensor --read --target="ultrasonic_emitter_01"

# Expected Output: STATUS: ACTIVE (Frequency: 45kHz)
# If INACTIVE, restart the daemon:
systemctl restart tank-security.service

```
